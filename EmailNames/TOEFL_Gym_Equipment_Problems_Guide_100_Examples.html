A reliable sentence formula

The [part] of the [equipment] is / keeps [problem], which makes it [effect].

Example: The belt on the treadmill keeps slipping, which makes the machine unsafe to use.

Give useful detail

  • Name the exact equipment.
  • Name the part when possible.
  • Describe what happens, not only what it looks like.
  • Explain the effect on safety or use.
  • End with a reasonable request.

Problem and damage vocabulary

Click any problem word to search Google for “what does X mean.” Start with general words, then become more specific when the defect is clear.

General condition

broken

not working or physically separated into pieces

Common use: a broken treadmill; a broken handle

damaged

harmed in a way that reduces quality, safety, or usefulness

Common use: damaged padding; a damaged frame

faulty

not working correctly because of a problem

Common use: a faulty sensor; faulty controls

defective

made with a fault or unable to work as intended

Common use: a defective cable; a defective safety lock

malfunctioning

operating incorrectly or unpredictably

Common use: a malfunctioning machine; a malfunctioning display

out of order

temporarily unavailable because it does not work

Common use: The stair climber is out of order.

unusable

too damaged, unsafe, or ineffective to be used

Common use: an unusable bench; the mat is unusable

Surface and material damage

cracked

having a narrow break in a hard surface

Common use: a cracked screen; cracked plastic

chipped

having a small piece broken off an edge or surface

Common use: a chipped weight plate; chipped paint

dented

having a hollow mark caused by an impact

Common use: a dented frame; a dented cover

bent

forced out of its normal straight shape

Common use: a bent barbell; a bent pin

warped

twisted or curved out of its correct shape

Common use: a warped footplate; a warped mat

torn

split or pulled apart, especially fabric, rubber, or padding

Common use: torn upholstery; a torn band

ripped

badly torn, often with a visible opening

Common use: a ripped seat cover; a ripped mat

frayed

having loose or worn threads or fibers at the edge

Common use: a frayed cable; frayed stitching

peeling

coming away from the surface in thin pieces or layers

Common use: peeling rubber; peeling padding

scratched

marked by thin lines caused by rubbing or contact

Common use: a scratched display; scratched paint

snapped

broken suddenly into two or more pieces

Common use: a snapped band; a snapped adjustment pin

stretched out

made longer or looser through repeated use

Common use: a stretched-out band; stretched-out straps

worn out

damaged through long or heavy use

Common use: worn-out pedals; a worn-out belt

rusty

covered with rust

Common use: rusty bolts; a rusty frame

corroded

gradually damaged by a chemical reaction, often involving metal

Common use: corroded connectors; corroded metal

Movement and mechanical problems

loose

not firmly fixed in place

Common use: a loose seat; loose bolts

unstable

not firm or safe; likely to move or fall

Common use: an unstable bench; an unstable rack

wobbly

moving slightly from side to side

Common use: a wobbly handle; a wobbly seat

stuck

unable to move from a position

Common use: a stuck adjustment pin; a stuck pedal

jammed

blocked so that a mechanism cannot move properly

Common use: a jammed pulley; a jammed sled

slipping

moving out of position when it should remain secure

Common use: a slipping treadmill belt; slipping foot straps

misaligned

not positioned correctly in relation to another part

Common use: misaligned guide rails; a misaligned belt

uneven

not level, smooth, or equal on both sides

Common use: an uneven base; uneven padding

squeaky

making a short, high sound during movement

Common use: squeaky pedals; a squeaky hinge

grinding

making a rough sound because parts are rubbing badly

Common use: a grinding noise; grinding pulleys

vibrating

shaking rapidly while operating

Common use: a vibrating treadmill; a vibrating display

leaking

allowing liquid or air to escape

Common use: a leaking hydraulic cylinder; leaking lubricant

Controls, electronics, and performance

unresponsive

not reacting when a button, screen, or control is used

Common use: an unresponsive screen; unresponsive buttons

frozen

stuck on one screen and unable to continue

Common use: a frozen display; a frozen timer

flickering

repeatedly becoming bright and dim or switching on and off

Common use: a flickering screen; flickering lights

inaccurate

showing information that is not correct

Common use: an inaccurate heart-rate sensor; an inaccurate counter

overheating

becoming hotter than is safe or normal

Common use: an overheating motor; an overheating console

cutting out

stopping suddenly and then sometimes starting again

Common use: The power keeps cutting out.

noisy

producing more sound than is normal or acceptable

Common use: a noisy rower; a noisy motor

slow to respond

reacting after an unusual delay

Common use: slow-to-respond controls; a delayed display

Cleanliness, completeness, and safety

dirty

covered with dirt, dust, sweat, or marks

Common use: dirty handles; a dirty exercise mat

sticky

unpleasantly tacky to the touch

Common use: sticky grips; a sticky seat

clogged

blocked by dirt or material

Common use: a clogged vent; clogged guide rails

missing

not present where it should be

Common use: a missing selector pin; missing screws

poorly secured

not attached firmly or safely

Common use: a poorly secured handle; poorly secured padding

sharp

having an exposed edge that could cut someone

Common use: a sharp metal edge; a sharp crack

exposed

not covered or protected as it should be

Common use: exposed wiring; an exposed cable

slippery

too smooth or wet to provide a safe grip

Common use: slippery pedals; a slippery mat

Useful language for a TOEFL email

Combine these phrases with the equipment and problem vocabulary above.

Introduce the issue

  • I am writing to report several problems with the gym equipment.
  • I noticed that some of the equipment is not working properly.
  • There are a few maintenance issues that may require attention.

Explain the effect

  • This makes the machine difficult to use.
  • This could cause someone to lose balance.
  • As a result, members may not be able to exercise safely.
  • The problem becomes worse at higher speeds.

Request action

  • Could you please have it inspected?
  • I recommend taking the machine out of service temporarily.
  • It may be necessary to replace the damaged part.
  • I would appreciate it if the equipment could be repaired soon.

Add precision

  • Degree: slightly, badly, severely
  • Frequency: occasionally, repeatedly, intermittently
  • Condition: whenever the speed increases
  • Timing: after a few minutes of use

Be specific rather than dramatic

Too vague: The gym equipment is really bad and dangerous.

Clearer: The seat on one exercise bike is loose, and the treadmill belt slips when the speed increases.

100 natural example sentences

These examples use the equipment, parts, and problem terms introduced above. They progress from simple reports to more precise descriptions and polite requests.

1. Clear basic reports

  1. One of the treadmills is broken and will not start.
  2. The exercise bike is out of order, so members cannot use it.
  3. The display on the elliptical machine is unresponsive.
  4. The rowing machine is noisy even at a low resistance level.
  5. The stair climber has a faulty speed control.
  6. The weight bench is unstable and moves from side to side.
  7. Two of the dumbbells are damaged.
  8. The barbell is bent and should not be used.
  9. Several weight plates have chipped edges.
  10. The squat rack has loose bolts near the base.
  11. The cable machine is malfunctioning.
  12. The leg press machine is jammed halfway through its movement.
  13. The seat on the chest press machine is stuck.
  14. The lat pulldown machine has a missing selector pin.
  15. One of the resistance bands is torn.
  16. Several exercise mats are dirty and sticky.

2. More precise descriptions

  1. The treadmill belt is worn out and has begun to fray along one edge.
  2. The emergency-stop button on the treadmill is cracked and slow to respond.
  3. The seat on the exercise bike is loose, and the adjustment lever does not hold it in place.
  4. One pedal strap on the exercise bike is ripped, making it difficult to secure the foot.
  5. The left pedal on the elliptical machine feels uneven and makes a squeaky sound.
  6. The moving handle on the elliptical machine is wobbly because one of its bolts is loose.
  7. The rowing machine has a frayed strap near the handle.
  8. The seat of the rowing machine keeps getting stuck on the rail.
  9. The handrail on the stair climber is poorly secured.
  10. The display on the stair climber is flickering and shows inaccurate speed information.
  11. The back pad on the weight bench is torn, and some of the foam is exposed.
  12. The adjustment pin on the weight bench is bent and difficult to remove.
  13. The rubber coating on several dumbbells is peeling.
  14. One dumbbell has a loose weight head, which could be dangerous.
  15. The sleeve on the barbell is stuck and no longer rotates smoothly.
  16. The center hole of one weight plate is chipped and uneven.

3. Effects, behavior, and safety

  1. The treadmill belt keeps slipping whenever the speed increases.
  2. The treadmill begins vibrating heavily after a few minutes of use.
  3. The exercise bike makes a grinding noise when the resistance is increased.
  4. The exercise bike monitor is frozen on the welcome screen.
  5. The elliptical machine keeps cutting out in the middle of a workout.
  6. The rowing machine shows an inaccurate distance on its monitor.
  7. The stair climber motor appears to be overheating after short periods of use.
  8. The weight bench has an uneven base, so it does not remain level.
  9. The barbell has a sharp, chipped section near the end of the shaft.
  10. Several weight plates are rusty and leave marks on users’ hands.
  11. The safety bars on the squat rack are misaligned and do not lock at the same height.
  12. The pull-up bar on the squat rack is loose and shifts under pressure.
  13. The cable on the cable machine is frayed and may snap during use.
  14. One pulley on the cable machine is jammed, causing the cable to move unevenly.
  15. The leg press footplate is slippery and needs to be cleaned.
  16. The safety lock on the leg press machine appears defective and sometimes fails to engage.

4. Polite requests and recommended action

  1. The handles on the chest press machine are sticky, so they should be cleaned before the next class.
  2. The chest press seat is wobbly, which makes it difficult to maintain a safe position.
  3. The pulldown bar on the lat pulldown machine is bent slightly to one side.
  4. The cable on the lat pulldown machine makes a grinding sound as it passes over the pulley.
  5. Several resistance bands are stretched out and no longer provide enough resistance.
  6. The stitching around one resistance-band handle is frayed and poorly secured.
  7. The corners of the exercise mats are warped, causing them to lift off the floor.
  8. One exercise mat is ripped and has a sharp piece of material exposed underneath.
  9. Could you please arrange for the malfunctioning treadmill to be inspected as soon as possible?
  10. I recommend replacing the torn pedal strap on the exercise bike before anyone uses it again.
  11. The unresponsive display on the elliptical machine may need to be repaired or replaced.
  12. It would be safer to remove the rowing machine from service until its frayed strap is replaced.
  13. Please have the loose handrail on the stair climber tightened.
  14. The unstable weight bench should be clearly marked as out of order.
  15. Because the cable machine has exposed wiring, members should be prevented from using it.
  16. I would appreciate it if the damaged equipment could be repaired before the gym becomes busy this weekend.

5. Additional equipment-and-part combinations

  1. The ventilation slots on the treadmill console are clogged with dust, and the motor begins overheating after about ten minutes.
  2. The rubber coating on one treadmill handrail is peeling, leaving an uncomfortable surface to grip.
  3. The right pedal on the exercise bike is bent inward and rubs against the frame.
  4. The resistance control on the exercise bike is slow to respond, so changing levels takes several seconds.
  5. The left pedal on the elliptical machine is cracked near the point where it connects to the arm.
  6. The elliptical machine has become increasingly squeaky and begins vibrating at higher resistance levels.
  7. The rowing machine monitor keeps flickering and displays an inaccurate stroke count.
  8. One of the foot straps on the rowing machine has snapped and can no longer secure the user’s foot.
  9. The left step on the stair climber is misaligned and sits lower than the right one.
  10. The emergency-stop key on the stair climber is missing.
  11. The metal frame of the weight bench is dented near the adjustment mechanism.
  12. The backrest of the weight bench appears warped and no longer lies evenly against the frame.
  13. The handle of one dumbbell is corroded and has developed a rough surface.
  14. The rubber end of another dumbbell is cracked and has begun peeling away in small pieces.
  15. One collar on the barbell is poorly secured and becomes loose during use.
  16. Several weight plates are rusty around their center holes and are difficult to slide onto the bar.
  17. The coating on the squat rack is badly scratched, and the exposed metal has started to corrode.
  18. A safety pin is missing from the left side of the squat rack.
  19. The guide rods on the cable machine are dirty, causing the weight stack to get stuck.
  20. The cover over one cable-machine pulley is cracked and leaves part of the moving mechanism exposed.

6. Developed, email-ready descriptions

  1. The leg press machine vibrates noticeably whenever the sled approaches the top of the track.
  2. A sharp metal edge is exposed along the lower corner of the leg press footplate.
  3. The covering on the chest press handles is peeling and feels sticky after it is cleaned.
  4. The resistance selector on the chest press machine is jammed and cannot be moved beyond the fourth setting.
  5. The seat pad on the lat pulldown machine is ripped, and the foam underneath is exposed.
  6. The cable on the lat pulldown machine is misaligned and has begun to fray where it rubs against the pulley.
  7. Most of the lighter resistance bands are stretched out, so they provide almost no resistance.
  8. One resistance band snapped during light use, which suggests that the remaining bands should also be inspected.
  9. The surface of one exercise mat is worn out and becomes slippery when it is damp.
  10. Two exercise mats are warped at the edges, creating a possible tripping hazard.
  11. Because the rowing machine monitor is frozen and unresponsive, users cannot select a workout or view their results.
  12. Since the left pedal on the exercise bike is poorly secured, I recommend marking the machine as out of order.
  13. Although the treadmill still runs, its display provides inaccurate information about both speed and distance.
  14. The selector pin on the cable machine appears defective and occasionally slips out of the weight stack.
  15. Could you please inspect the unstable weight bench, the malfunctioning stair climber, and the damaged resistance bands before the next training session?
  16. I would appreciate it if the broken and unsafe equipment could be removed from service until the faulty parts have been repaired or replaced.

Mini model: one developed paragraph

I am writing to report several problems with the equipment in the gym. First, the belt on one treadmill keeps slipping whenever the speed increases, which could cause a user to lose balance. In addition, the seat on one exercise bike is loose, and its adjustment lever does not hold it firmly in place. I also noticed that the cable on the lat pulldown machine is frayed near the pulley. This equipment may be unsafe to use until the damaged parts are repaired. Could you please arrange for these machines to be inspected and clearly mark them as out of order in the meantime?
