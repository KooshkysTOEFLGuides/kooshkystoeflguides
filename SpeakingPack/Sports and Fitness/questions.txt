# SET 1 — EVERYDAY PARTICIPATION

**Interview setup:**
You have volunteered for a university research study about sports and physical activity. You will answer a few questions in a short online interview.

**Q1.** Thank you for agreeing to participate. I’d like to ask you some questions about sports and physical activity. First, do you currently play a sport or do any kind of exercise? How often?

**Q2.** Thank you. Where do you usually exercise or take part in this activity, and what do you normally do there?

**Q3.** Interesting. Has there ever been a period when you were more or less physically active than you are now? What caused the change?

**Q4.** Finally, some people think everyone should make time for regular exercise, even if they do not enjoy sports. What do you think?

# SET 2 — TEAM OR INDIVIDUAL SPORTS

**Interview setup:**
You have agreed to take part in a university study about people’s sports preferences. A researcher will interview you online.

**Q1.** Thank you for joining the study. To begin, do you prefer team sports or individual sports? Why?

**Q2.** I see. Can you describe a team or individual sport that you are familiar with? What do participants need to do?

**Q3.** Moving on, what might make someone enjoy exercising alone rather than being part of a team?

**Q4.** For my final question, should schools require students to play team sports, or should students be free to choose individual activities instead?

# SET 3 — LEARNING A NEW SPORT

**Interview setup:**
You have volunteered for a short research interview about learning and trying new sports. The interview is part of a university project.

**Q1.** Thank you for participating. Is there a sport or physical activity that you have never tried but would like to learn?

**Q2.** Great. What does that activity involve, and what makes it attractive to you?

**Q3.** Interesting. What could prevent you from starting or continuing that sport?

**Q4.** Some people prefer to become highly skilled at one sport, while others enjoy trying many different activities. Which approach do you prefer, and why?

# SET 4 — EXERCISE ROUTINES

**Interview setup:**
You have signed up for a university study about exercise habits. You will have a short video interview with one of the researchers.

**Q1.** Thank you for taking part. Do you prefer to exercise at a fixed time, or do you exercise whenever you have free time?

**Q2.** Thank you. Can you describe what a typical exercise session would look like for you?

**Q3.** I see. What usually causes people to miss exercise sessions or give up a routine?

**Q4.** Finally, which do you think is more effective: following a strict exercise schedule or using a flexible routine?

# SET 5 — DIFFERENT KINDS OF FITNESS

**Interview setup:**
You have agreed to answer questions for a research project about physical fitness. A graduate student will conduct the interview online.

**Q1.** Thank you for agreeing to participate. Which would you most like to improve: your strength, stamina, flexibility, balance, or another physical ability?

**Q2.** What activity or exercise do you think would help you improve that ability?

**Q3.** Interesting. Can you tell me about someone you know who became noticeably fitter or stronger? What did that person do?

**Q4.** Some people judge fitness mainly by physical appearance, while others focus on what the body is able to do. Which view makes more sense to you?

# SET 6 — MEMORABLE EXPERIENCES

**Interview setup:**
You are participating in a university study about people’s experiences with sports and exercise. The researcher will ask you four questions online.

**Q1.** Thank you for joining the study. Can you tell me about a sports or exercise experience that you remember especially well?

**Q2.** Thank you. What happened before or during the activity that made the experience memorable?

**Q3.** Looking back, did you learn anything from that experience about yourself or other people?

**Q4.** Some people think competition helps people perform better, while others think it creates too much stress. What is your opinion?

# SET 7 — INDOOR AND OUTDOOR ACTIVITIES

**Interview setup:**

**Q1.** Thank you for participating. Do you prefer indoor activities or outdoor activities? Why?

**Q2.** Can you describe an ideal place where you would enjoy exercising or playing a sport?

**Q3.** Interesting. How can weather, location, or safety affect a person’s choice of physical activity?

**Q4.** If a community had money for only one project, should it build an indoor fitness center or improve outdoor parks and sports areas?

# SET 8 — MOTIVATION AND OBSTACLES

**Interview setup:**
You have agreed to take part in a short university interview about motivation to exercise. The interview will be conducted online.

**Q1.** Thank you for helping with our study. What usually motivates you, or people you know, to become more physically active?

**Q2.** Imagine that a friend wants to exercise but dislikes going to gyms. What activity would you recommend?

**Q3.** I see. What challenge might make it difficult for that person to continue, and how could the person deal with it?

**Q4.** Some people reward themselves after reaching an exercise goal. Do you think rewards are a useful way to build a lasting habit?

# SET 12 — WATCHING SPORTS

# SET 13 — COST AND ACCESS

**Q2.** Can you describe an affordable physical activity that people can do where you live?

# SET 14 — FITNESS TECHNOLOGY

**Interview setup:**
You have volunteered for a university project about technology and physical activity. A graduate student will interview you online.

**Q1.** Thank you for participating. How important is technology in the way you exercise or learn about fitness?

**Q2.** Fitness technology can include exercise apps, online classes, or wearable trackers. Which of these would be most useful to you, and why?

**Q3.** Interesting. What problems can occur when people pay too much attention to numbers such as steps, calories, or workout time?

**Q4.** In the future, do you think people will exercise mainly through online programs, or will gyms and sports clubs remain popular?

# SET 15 — HEALTH AND APPEARANCE

**Interview setup:**
You have agreed to take part in a university study about people’s ideas of health and fitness. The researcher will ask you four questions online.

**Q1.** Thank you for agreeing to participate. What does being physically fit mean to you?

**Q2.** Can you describe one change a person could make to become healthier without completely changing their lifestyle?

# SET 16 — SPORTS THROUGHOUT LIFE

**Interview setup:**
You have volunteered for a university research study about how exercise habits change over time. You will have a short online interview.

**Q1.** Thank you for taking part. Has your interest in sports or exercise changed as you have grown older?

**Q2.** Can you describe how the physical activities of an older family member differ from those of younger people?

**Q3.** Interesting. Why do many people become less physically active after they leave school or begin working full time?

**Q4.** Looking ahead, do you think future generations will be more physically active or less physically active than people are today?

# Corpus-Calibration Audit

* **Set 1:** Q1—current participation and frequency; Q2—concrete routine and location; Q3—change over time; Q4—broader opinion about regular exercise. Subtopics: participation, routines, personal history, health.

* **Set 2:** Q1—personal preference; Q2—description of a familiar sport; Q3—reason for exercising alone; Q4—school choice. Subtopics: team and individual sports, descriptions, personality, education.

* **Set 3:** Q1—hypothetical personal choice; Q2—concrete description and attraction; Q3—obstacle; Q4—comparison of specialization and variety. Subtopics: types of sports, skill development, barriers, preferences.

* **Set 4:** Q1—routine preference; Q2—description of a session; Q3—causes of inconsistency; Q4—comparison of strict and flexible routines. Subtopics: exercise habits, participation, time, motivation.

* **Set 5:** Q1—personal fitness goal; Q2—suitable activity; Q3—example of improvement; Q4—broader judgment about fitness. Subtopics: strength, stamina, flexibility, fitness experiences, body function.

* **Set 6:** Q1—memorable experience; Q2—concrete development; Q3—lesson learned; Q4—effect of competition. Subtopics: experiences, participation, personal growth, pressure.

* **Set 7:** Q1—indoor or outdoor preference; Q2—description of a place; Q3—practical obstacles; Q4—accessible community choice. Subtopics: sport types, facilities, weather, community access.

* **Set 8:** Q1—motivation; Q2—realistic recommendation; Q3—obstacle and response; Q4—general judgment about rewards. Subtopics: participation, motivation, exercise programs, habit formation.

* **Set 9:** Q1—childhood experience; Q2—recommendation for a child; Q3—parental influence; Q4—opinion about contact sports. Subtopics: experiences, sport choice, children, contact versus noncontact sports.

* **Set 10:** Q1—familiar school experience; Q2—concrete example; Q3—cause of discomfort; Q4—educational priority. Subtopics: school sports, emotions, participation, competition.

* **Set 11:** Q1—social preference; Q2—description of another person’s routine; Q3—relationship effect; Q4—school and workplace activities. Subtopics: individual and group exercise, family, friendship, institutions.

* **Set 12:** Q1—spectator habits; Q2—real or hypothetical event; Q3—cause of fan loyalty; Q4—broader opinion about athletes. Subtopics: watching sports, live events, sports culture, role models.

* **Set 13:** Q1—equipment preference; Q2—concrete affordable activity; Q3—access barriers; Q4—public-facility opinion. Subtopics: equipment, cost, transportation, accessibility.

* **Set 14:** Q1—personal use of technology; Q2—choice among familiar tools; Q3—possible disadvantage; Q4—future prediction. Subtopics: exercise technology, online fitness, habits, future change.

* **Set 15:** Q1—personal definition; Q2—practical health change; Q3—social-media effect; Q4—comparison of motivations. Subtopics: fitness, health, body image, enjoyment, long-term habits.

* **Set 16:** Q1—personal change over time; Q2—generational description; Q3—cause of declining participation; Q4—future prediction. Subtopics: age, family, work, participation, future trends.

* The Listen and Repeat portions of the corpus were ignored.

* Every set contains exactly four Interview questions.

* Every set samples several broad angles rather than investigating one narrow technical issue.

* Question 1 is consistently personal, familiar, or immediately accessible.

* Question 2 is primarily concrete, descriptive, comparative, or realistically hypothetical.

* Question 3 provides flexible development through an experience, cause, obstacle, effect, or recommendation.

* Question 4 broadens the topic while remaining accessible to an ordinary university-age test taker.

* No question requires knowledge of sports medicine, professional coaching, exercise science, or specialized sports administration.

* No set was added merely to represent one minor item from the coverage map.

* The questions use generic prerecorded transitions that do not assume what the participant previously said.

* The wording follows the underlying design of the corpus while correcting its transcription, punctuation, and grammatical problems.
